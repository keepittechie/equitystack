# EquityStack Outreach Priority Pages

Internal working document for deciding which public pages are worth promoting externally first, to whom, and in what context.

This is not a claim that EquityStack already has broad outside authority. It is a practical map of the pages that are currently most ready to earn links, citations, and serious shares.

## How To Use This Map

- Use `Wave 1` pages first for external sharing, outreach, and public reference.
- Use `Wave 2` pages when the audience is more specialized or when the topic needs a narrower angle.
- Treat `Hold / Improve` pages as useful internally, but not yet strong enough for broad promotion without more editorial depth.

## First-Wave Promotion Recommendation

If promotion started now, the first wave should focus on these 10 pages:

1. `/research`
2. `/methodology`
3. `/reports/black-impact-score`
4. `/reports/civil-rights-timeline`
5. `/analysis/presidential-impact-on-black-americans`
6. `/analysis/civil-rights-laws-by-president`
7. `/analysis/presidents-and-black-americans`
8. `/explainers/equal-protection-under-the-law`
9. `/explainers/redlining-black-homeownership`
10. `/explainers/gi-bill-access-and-impact`

Why this first wave:

- It balances broad entry pages, flagship trust pages, report-level summary pages, and issue explainers.
- It avoids over-indexing on thinner record pages.
- It gives outside audiences several valid entry points depending on whether they need orientation, synthesis, law, or context.

## Current Weak Spots

These are the main reasons not to push the entire site equally hard yet:

- Many non-flagship record pages are still thinner than the best thematic, report, and explainer pages.
- Some promise and policy records still depend too heavily on short record summaries.
- The strongest public pages now explain how to use the site well, but deeper record coverage still varies by topic and era.
- The site still lacks one dedicated internal/public page explaining exactly which EquityStack pages are best to cite for which task.

## Pages To Hold Back For Now

Useful pages, but not strong first-wave promotion targets:

- Most non-flagship policy pages
- Most non-flagship promise pages
- Most future-bill pages
- Glossary as a lead page
- Narrower thematic pages unless the outreach context clearly matches them

These pages are still valuable internally and as second-click destinations, but they are not the best “share this first” assets.

## Recommended Future Link Magnets

Not yet required for current promotion, but likely useful next:

- A dedicated `How to Cite and Use EquityStack` page
- Topic-curated resource pages such as:
  - best pages on housing and wealth
  - best pages on civil-rights law
  - best pages on criminal justice

- More authored flagship report pages beyond the current top report set
