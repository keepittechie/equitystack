# EquityStack Content Strategy

This document defines what strong public content looks like for the main public page types and how to handle thin-record risk without fabricating facts.

## Editorial Standards

All public content should be:

- neutral
- historically grounded
- research-oriented
- concise but useful
- explicit about limits

It should not become:

- advocacy copy
- ranking rhetoric
- generic SEO filler
- unsupported historical synthesis

## What Good Looks Like By Page Type

### Thematic analysis pages

Good thematic pages:

- state the question clearly
- explain what the page is for
- distinguish their role from adjacent thematic pages
- route users into records, reports, explainers, and methods

They should not try to answer every historical question directly.

### President detail pages

Good president pages:

- explain what the profile measures
- clarify the difference between profile summary and full historical judgment
- route users into policies, promises, bills, reports, and methodology

### Policy detail pages

Good policy pages:

- explain what happened
- explain why the record matters
- show evidence and completeness nearby
- connect to related promises, explainers, and thematic guides

### Promise detail pages

Good promise pages:

- separate statement, status, and evidence
- explain what to compare
- link into policies, president profile, reports, and methodology

### Report pages

Good report pages:

- explain what the report covers
- explain what the report does not do
- route into record pages and trust pages

### Explainer pages

Good explainers:

- explain the topic clearly
- frame why it matters
- connect to related records and thematic hubs
- avoid sounding like a blog post or opinion essay

## Thin Content Rules

When a record is thin:

- do not invent extra facts
- do not extrapolate beyond the visible record
- add context from:
  - title
  - page type
  - linked counts
  - related records
  - visible evidence/completeness signals
- explain how to use the page responsibly

## Lightweight Quality Scoring

Use this 1-5 heuristic when evaluating a page type:

- `Summary depth`: Does the page explain itself clearly beyond the headline?
- `Internal linking`: Does it route into the right next pages?
- `Clarity`: Can a first-time visitor tell what the page is for?
- `Usefulness`: Would a serious reader get real value before leaving?

## Summary Writing Guidelines

Aim for:

- 2-4 compact paragraphs when the underlying record supports it
- one paragraph on what the page covers
- one paragraph on why it matters
- one paragraph on how to interpret the page responsibly

Avoid:

- summary paragraphs that repeat the title without adding information
- empty “importance” language without concrete page-level context
- generic calls to action that do not explain what the next page offers
